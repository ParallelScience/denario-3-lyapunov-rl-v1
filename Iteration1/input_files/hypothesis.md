The performance instability and "gradient traps" observed in previous iterations are caused by the rigid, fixed contribution of the Lyapunov function $\Phi(s)$ to the value estimate, which ignores the discount factor $\gamma$ and the specific dynamics of the Pendulum-v1 environment. I hypothesize that introducing a learned, state-dependent scaling factor $\alpha(s)$—parameterized as a small neural network—to the Lyapunov prior, such that $V(s) = \alpha(s)\Phi(s) + f_\theta(s)$, will allow the critic to dynamically modulate the influence of the physical prior. By constraining $\alpha(s)$ to be positive (e.g., via a Softplus activation) and initializing it near 1, the model can "attenuate" the Lyapunov prior in regions of the state space where the physical energy $\Phi(s)$ is a poor proxy for the discounted return, thereby resolving the optimization conflict while maintaining the structural benefits of the physical prior.
