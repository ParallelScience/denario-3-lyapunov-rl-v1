The current failure to maintain upright stability stems from the "telescoping" nature of the potential-based reward, which makes the cumulative return invariant to the path taken, effectively removing the incentive to stay at the goal once reached. I hypothesize that by combining the structured critic $V(s) = \Phi(s) + f_\theta(s)$ with a **Hybrid Reward Function**—defined as $R_{total} = R_{native} + \beta \cdot (\Phi(s_t) - \Phi(s_{t+1}))$—we can leverage the Lyapunov gradient to accelerate initial convergence while using the native reward to provide the necessary "stay-at-goal" incentive. Furthermore, I propose introducing a **Dynamic Weighting Factor ($\beta$)** that decays as the agent approaches the goal state ($\Phi(s) < \epsilon$), allowing the agent to transition from a Lyapunov-guided exploration phase to a native-reward-driven exploitation phase. This approach preserves the structural benefits of the prior while correcting the policy's inability to maintain long-term stability.
