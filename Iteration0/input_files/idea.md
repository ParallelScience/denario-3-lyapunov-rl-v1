**Title: Adaptive Residual Learning for Compensating Systematic Dynamics Mismatch**

This research investigates the efficacy of the structured value function $V(s) = \Phi(s) + f_\theta(s)$ in accelerating policy convergence when the pendulum's underlying dynamics are systematically biased by an unmodeled constant torque disturbance $\tau_{ext} \in \{0.5, 1.0, 1.5\}$ Nm. Since the analytical Lyapunov prior $\Phi(s)$ is derived from the nominal pendulum physics, it fails to account for the equilibrium displacement caused by $\tau_{ext}$, forcing the residual network $f_\theta(s)$ to explicitly learn the state-dependent correction required to restore the upright equilibrium at $\theta=0$. We compare this structured approach against a monolithic SAC critic to determine if the physical prior acts as a stable anchor that reduces the search space for the residual network, thereby increasing sample efficiency. Performance is evaluated by measuring the convergence rate to 90% of the maximum Lyapunov reward and the precision of the upright stabilization under these shifted dynamics. This framework demonstrates how a structured critic leverages physical domain knowledge to generalize across environments with persistent, non-zero-mean dynamic offsets that would otherwise destabilize standard value learning.
